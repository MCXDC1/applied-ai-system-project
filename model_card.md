Reflection and Ethics: Thinking Critically About Your AI:

What are the limitations or biases in your system?
    - My system is not educated on more exotic pets, meaning that the system could be misinformed and give wrong information for other animals. 
Could your AI be misused, and how would you prevent that?
    - The AI could most likely be misused. I have attemted to place guardrails into place to make sure that users cannot give dangerous information such as feeding dogs chocolate. However, there is still a possibility that a user could somehow place a task into the system without catching it. The system is recommending a calender based on what is put into profiles so best case, information is checked regularly.
What surprised you while testing your AI's reliability?
    - I was surprised at how much it was able to catch before I placed guardrails in. The system was able to reliably catch a lot of dangerous information on food and habits.
Describe your collaboration with AI during this project. Identify one instance when the AI gave a helpful suggestion and one instance where its suggestion was flawed or incorrect.
    - AI was a large part of the creation of the foundation of the project. Through this I was able to guide the system's building blocks on how I wanted it to be built. I was then able to add features that would make it useful for its purpose. AI suggested a very strong RAG engine, which was double checked after and then had more information added on. AI was not helpful in terms of ease of use in the website itself. I had to reject UI suggestions.
